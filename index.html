Worked at Microsoft (company) (2009–2011) . Updated 3y

How has Microsoft avoided the big tech backlash from government that Google, Facebook, Amazon, and Apple are experiencing? Is it less a consumer company than its peers (not dependent on the harvesting of personal data like Facebook and Google are)?

Microsoft doesn't forget. Compared to many tech companies, people who do well at Microsoft don't leave very often. In fact, many people say you aren't considered established at the company for about 5 years. That's eons at a place like Amazon.

In the late 1990s, Microsoft was sued by the Justice Department for antitrust violations and anti-competitive behavior. The penalties and sanctions were very large and very painful. When I worked there ten years later, that memory was still very fresh among the veterans who lived through it.

Many of those people are still at the company. Microsoft is a very disciplined (if sometimes odd) corporate culture. It remembers its lore, the good and the bad, and they drill those lessons into new employees so the same mistakes aren't repeated.

I also found that Microsoft was extremely protective of customer data. I worked in online services, and Microsoft would frequently push back on overly broad requests from governments for customer data, even when there was a subpoena. Microsoft was also one of the few companies that fought the government and the intelligence community's mass surveillance programs all the way to the top of the judicial system in appeal after appeal in the FISA courts.

People can bag on Redmond all they like, but MSFT is one of the few companies that takes its security systems and customer data very, very seriously.

They are meticulous about how they compete, and compared to many companies in tech, they move a lot slower because they've experienced the consequences of “winning at all costs” or “move fast and break things”. Recklessness isn't a habit for companies that think in decades. Experience is a good teacher.
